# 📊 Employee Attrition Analysis Dashboard (SQL + Power BI)

## 🧑‍💼 Project Overview
This project analyzes employee attrition patterns using HR data to understand why employees are leaving the organization and to provide actionable insights for improving employee retention.

The solution is built using:
- SQL (for data aggregation using views)
- Power BI (for visualization and dashboard creation)

---

## 🎯 Business Problem
The organization is facing increasing employee attrition and wants to understand:
- What is the current attrition rate?
- Which departments and job roles have higher attrition?
- What are the key factors driving employee attrition?
- How can employee retention be improved?

---

## 📌 Business Requirements

### 🟦 1. Attrition Overview (What is happening?)
The company wants to monitor overall employee attrition.

**Requirements:**
- Calculate total number of employees
- Calculate total attrition count
- Calculate attrition rate (%)
- Identify attrition distribution by:
  - Department
  - Job Level

---

### 🟩 2. Root Cause Analysis (Why is it happening?)
The company wants to identify key factors influencing attrition.

**Requirements:**
- Analyze attrition based on:
  - Monthly Income (Salary)
  - Job Satisfaction
  - Overtime
- Identify patterns between working conditions and employee exits

---

### 🟨 3. Employee Insights & Action Plan (What should be done?)
The company wants to identify high-risk employee groups and take corrective action.

**Requirements:**
- Analyze attrition by:
  - Age groups
  - Years at Company (Experience)
  - Promotion history
- Identify employee segments with high attrition risk
- Provide actionable recommendations to reduce attrition

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies Used
- SQL (Views, Aggregations, CASE statements)
- Power BI (Dashboard & Visualization)
- Data Modeling (Star-like analysis structure)
- Excel / CSV dataset

---

## 📊 Dataset Information
- Dataset contains employee demographic and work-related attributes such as:
  - Age
  - Department
  - Job Level
  - Monthly Income
  - Job Satisfaction
  - OverTime
  - Years at Company
  - Promotion history
  - Attrition status

---

## 🧠 Key SQL Concepts Used
- GROUP BY
- CASE WHEN statements
- Aggregate functions (COUNT, SUM)
- Views for structured reporting

---

## 📈 Key Insights from Analysis
- Employees with low salary show higher attrition
- Employees working overtime are more likely to leave
- Lack of promotion increases attrition rate
- New employees have higher turnover compared to experienced employees
- Certain departments show significantly higher attrition rates

---

## 🚀 Outcome
This project helps HR teams:
- Understand employee turnover patterns
- Identify risk factors affecting retention
- Make data-driven HR decisions
